Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • The novel begins on a rainy day in November at the home of the wealth Reed family known as Gateshead
  • A little girl, Jane Eyre, sits alone in the drawing room of the house reading a book on British Birds
  • Jane has been forbidden by playing with the other children in the house, her cousins Eliza, Georgiana, and John, by her aunt Mrs. Reed
  • Jane is an orphan and only lives in the house due to the charity of the mother of the other children and John makes sure to make fun of Jane for being an orphan
  • While Jane is reading John harasses her and throws a book at her causing her to lash out, the two fight, yet Jane is the one punished
  • Jane is sent to the "red-room", a frightening place where Uncle Reed had died
  • Chapter 2
  • Jane is led to the red-room by two servants, Miss Abbott and Bessie Lee
  • Jane tries to put up a fight but ultimately succumbs to their strength and enters the room
  • While in the room Jane notices her appearance in the mirror, she is very pail and frail looking which seems to surprise her
  • During the time alone she reflects on the events leading to her current state
  • She thinks back to her kind Uncle Reed taking her in and bringing her to Gateshead after her parents had died, however not long after he died himself
  • Jane begins to feel the presence of Uncle Reed in the room with her
  • She believes he has come to avenge the way Jane has been treated by his wife
  • As the idea of the ghost seems more real Jane cries out in terror but is ignored, she eventually passes out in fear
  • Chapter 3
  • Jane awakes from her fainting spell in her own bed at the care of Mr. Lloyd, a caretaker at the Gateshead house
  • Bessie has also joined Jane in the room and comments on how Mrs. Reed has been very unfair
  • Bessie takes care of Jane and sings to her to comfort her during throughout the day after she fainted
  • Mr. Lloyd has a suggestion that he presents to both Jane and her aunt, he thinks it would be wise to send Jane away to school
  • The idea seems to thrill Jane as she imagines what life would be like away from Gateshead
  • A few days following this Jane over hears Bessie and Miss Abbott discussing Jane and her dead parents
  • Jane learns that her mother was part of the Reed family and was initially entitled to inherit part of the Reed fortune
  • However after her mother met and married her father, a poor clergyman, she was written out of the will
  • It wasn't very long after Jane was born that they both died of typhus
  • Chapter 4
  • Two months have gone by since Jane overheard the conversation and Jane's life at Gateshead seems to have gotten much worse
  • Her aunt and cousins harass her daily and she becomes very anxious to learn whether or not she will be going away to school
  • Eventually she learns that she has been permitted to attend Lowood school, an all girls school ran by a stern man by the name of Mr. Brocklehurst
  • Jane meets the headmaster and he questions her profusely about religion and morals
  • He is not very pleased with her lack of interest in the psalms, and before he concludes her visit with her Mrs. Reed tells him that Jane has been known to lie often
  • Jane is very hurt by her aunt's accusations and defends herself
  • In the days before school Jane spends much of her time with Bessie, Bessie tells Jane that she prefers Jane to the Reed children, and Bessie tells her stories and sings her songs to pass the time
  • Chapter 5
  • A few days after Jane meets Mr. Brocklehurst she travels to Lowood to begin school
  • The building she moves into looks very grim but Jane settles in anyways and prepares for school the following day
  • The next morning Jane is taken to class and introduced to the other students and learns her schedule which will keep her occupied each day from dawn till supper time
  • During her first few days Jane is introduced to a very kind woman, Miss Temple, who is the superintendent of the school, she also gets to know one of her teachers Miss Scatcherd who turns out to be very unpleasant
  • Jane's first friend at Lowood is Helen Burns, Jane learns from Helen that Lowood is a charity school and that the school is for female orphans, therefore the Reed family paid nothing for her to attend
  • Chapter 6
  • During the early days of her time at Lowood Jane learns that life there wont be easy
  • One morning the girls cannot bathe because the water used for bathing is frozen, they are also not fed nearly enough, and are overworked
  • Jane however does see one positive about Lowood, her new friendship
  • Jane is very impressed by Helen and how she can endure the harsh treatment she receives from Miss Scathcherd without being upset
  • Helen explains that her Christian faith keeps her strong and she has learned to love and accept her enemies
  • Jane cannot comprehend how Helen lives that way but admires her all the same
  • The reader also learns that Helen is a very self-critical person and that she is often upset with herself for losing focus and not devoting her full attention to her studies
  • Chapter 7
  • Jane's first month of school passes and for the most part without too much trouble for Jane
  • However Mr. Brocklehurst who had been away from the school for that time finally returns and Jane worries that he will make good on the promise he made to Jane's aunt and inform the teachers that Jane is a habitual liar
  • One day while in school Jane accidentally drops her slate while Mr. Brocklehurst is present
  • Mr. Brocklehurst chastises her for being careless and punishes her by making her stand in a chair in front of the school while he informed the students and teachers of her supposed habit of lying
  • He then forbids the students from speaking to her for the rest of the day
  • Chapter 8
  • At the end of the school day Jane collapses to the floor in exhaustion from being an outcast at school
  • She is very embarrassed and believes her reputation at the school will never be the same
  • Helen tries to console her and tells her that most girls do not believe she is a liar and instead feel pity for her
  • Jane decides to tell her teacher Miss Temple that the accusations are false
  • Miss Temple seems to believe her and she decides to write back to Gateshead and Mr. Lloyd to confirm what Jane has said
  • Meanwhile back at school Jane and Helen spend time with Miss Temple and the three become closer
  • Eventually a letter returns from Mr. Lloyd stating that Jane is not a liar
  • Miss Temple is pleased and decides to pass the news onto the rest of the school
  • After regaining her reputation Jane begins to work very hard in school becoming quite the student
  • She also begins to excel in art and French as well
  • Chapter 9
  • It is now spring at school and for a while everything seems perfect for Jane
  • This is short lived however as a rash of Typhus breaks out throughout the school and many girls fall ill
  • Jane remains healthy however Helen does become sick, not with typhus but with consumption
  • Meanwhile Jane has made a new friend, Mary Ann Wilson, and the two spend much time playing together
  • Helen is dying and Jane goes to see her one last time, Helen explains that she does not feel pain and that she is happy to be moving on past her life on earth
  • That evening Helen dies in her sleep while she sleeps next to Jane
  • Helen is buried in an unmarked grave, however 15 years later the grave is covered by a marble tablet with the word Resurgam for "I shall rise again"
  • Chapter 10
  • After the typhus outbreak it is discovered that Mr. Brocklehurst's negligence as head of school lead to the students getting sick
  • He is replaced and the school becomes a much happier and better place to be
  • Jane spends the next six years studying at Lowood and excelling in school
  • She enjoys the school so much that she decides to spend two more years teaching but eventually she decides its time to move on
  • She begins to search for a job as a governess and eventually lands a job at a home known as Thornfield
  • Before Jane leaves Lowood for her knew job she is visited by Bessie who updates her on life at Gateshead
  • Georgiana had tried to run away with a man, Lord Edwin Vere, however she was unsuccessful as Eliza gave up the plan to Mrs. Reed
  • Meanwhile John has become a delinquent
  • Bessie also mentions that Jane's uncle from her fathers side had come looking for her a while back, however when he didn't find her at Gateshead he traveled on to Madeira, an island, in search of fortune
  • The two part ways at the end of their meeting, Jane to Thornfield, and Bessie back to Gateshead
  • Chapter 11
  • After a long day of travelling Jane arrives at Thornfield in the evening
  • Once inside she notices the house is quite cozy and seems like a nice place to live
  • Jane meets a woman known as Mrs. Fairfax, an elderly housekeeper
  • Jane learns that the owner of the house is a man by the name of Mr. Rochester, and that he spends most of his time traveling leaving the house in the care of Mrs. Fairfax
  • Jane then learns that she will be tutoring a young French girl, Adele, during her time at Thornfield
  • Mrs. Fairfax warns Jane about Mr. Rochester and explains that he is quite eccentric and can be violent at times
  • While they are wrapping up their conversation they are interrupted by loud and harsh laughter
  • Jane learns that it is coming from a rather odd woman, Grace, who works at the house as a seamstress
  • Chapter 12
  • Jane enjoys her new life and finds working with Adele quite pleasurable even though Adele at times can be rather annoying and childish
  • One evening a few months after arriving at Thornfield Jane watches the moon rise while alone
  • In the distance she sees a horse and is reminded of an eerie story Bessy told her about a spirit known as Gytrash, the spirit disguises itself as an animal and frightens travelers, Jane even sees a dog in the distance as well adding to her fright
  • However before she can be too frightened by the horse she notices that their is a rider on the horse
  • The horse happens to slip on a patch of ice and the rider is thrown from the horse causing Jane to go and help the rider
  • She introduces herself to the man helps him up
  • She then returns into Thornfield and inquires a servant about what she had seen
  • Jane learns that the man she helped up was in fact Mr. Rochester
  • Chapter 13
  • The day after Mr. Rochester returns home he invites Jane and Adele to join him for tea
  • Jane notices that he is a rather abrupt and cold man and that the only time he seems interested in Jane is when she shows him her drawings
  • Jane speaks with Mrs. Fairfax following her meeting with Mr. Rochester
  • Mrs. Fairfax explains that Mr. Rochester has become the way he is due to a rough personal history
  • He had been an outcast in his family for much of his life, and when his father died his brother and not him inherited Thornfield, however after his brothers death he came to live there
  • Chapter 14
  • Jane does not see Rochester very much during his first couple of days back at Thornfield, however one evening following dinner Rochester seeks out Jane and Adele
  • Rochester presents a gift to Adele which greatly excites her, and while Adele plays with her new gift Rochester has a conversation with Jane
  • Rochester is very playful at first but after asking Jane whether or not she finds him handsome and Jane's reply of "no" Rochester becomes aggressive and slightly angry
  • Jane begins to realize that he is drunk and the conversation from their on out seems very awkward to Jane as Rochester keeps suggesting that Jane's relationship to him is not one of servitude
  • Adele eventually joins the conversation and mentions her mother, however Rochester changes the subject and says that topic is for a later date
  • Chapter 15
  • Rochester does eventually decide to tell Jane about Adele's past
  • He explains that a while back he had an affair with Adele's mother who was a French singer and dancer by the name of Celine Varens
  • Rochester however learns that Celine was dating another man and their relationship ends
  • Celine had a child around the time of their relationship and claimed it was Rochester's, however he denied it because he looks nothing like Adele
  • Ironically though when Celine abandoned Adele, Rochester decided to adopt her and take her to England
  • Later that evening while Jane lays in bed contemplating the night she hears a strange laughter in the hallway
  • Interested, she seeks out the sound and instead discovers smoke leaving Rochester's room
  • Jane rushes in and discovers Rochester's bed curtains on fire
  • Jane manages to put the fire out and save Rochester
  • The two discuss what happened and Jane believes that it was Grace who set the bed ablaze
  • Rochester asks Jane to not tell anyone about what occurred and the two return to sleep
  • Chapter 16
  • The next morning Jane is shocked to learn that no one believes the events of the last night to be strange
  • The servants believe the fire is the result of a candle, and Grace seems to show no signs of remorse
  • In the meantime Jane seems to begin to feel the stirrings of attraction for Rochester which bothers her greatly
  • She is also upset to learn that Rochester will be away for a few days at a party for a woman known as Blanche Ingram
  • Jane knows she should not be upset but she cannot help it, she even compares herself to Blanche
  • Chapter 17
  • Rochester’s travels have left him away from Thornfield for a week, his absence leaves Jane slightly lonely and dismayed, and the possibility that he may be leaving to Europe without returning to Thornfield further dismays her
  • However after another week Jane learns that Rochester will in fact be returning home, this time with a group of guests
  • In the meantime Jane is shocked by the way Grace goes about life normally at Thornfield without any awkwardness with the rest of the staff despite her strange antics
  • Jane also learns that Grace earns quite a substantial salary leading Jane to wonder about her relationship to Thornfield and Rochester
  • Eventually Rochester returns home with the group of guests who all appear very wealthy and some quite snobbish
  • In particular Blanche Ingram and her mother are very stuck up and are quite rude to Jane
  • Jane becomes so bothered by the group that she tries to separate herself to Rochester's dismay
  • One evening when Jane heads off to bed to get away from the group Rochester tells her goodnight and nearly slips and says the phrase “my love”
  • Chapter 18
  • The guests have now graced Thornfield with their presence for quite a few days
  • Jane notices that Rochester and Blanche seem to appear slightly close, even playing charades together
  • Jane feels disappointed as she believes that Rochester and Blanche are bound to marry, Blanche for the money, and Rochester for the looks and social position, despite any appearances of feelings of love for one another
  • One evening a man, Mr. Mason, arrives to Thornfield and immediately Jane does not like him very much
  • Jane learns that Rochester used to live in the West Indies and that Mr. Mason has lived there as well
  • A few days later a fortune teller arrives to tell the guests their fortune, when Blanche learns hers she appears visibly disappointed
  • Chapter 19
  • Jane eventually has her fortune read, she is initially skeptical however she warms up to the idea shortly after speaking to the fortune teller
  • The fortune teller knows quite a bit about Jane, she also gives her insight into Blanche's reading and tells Jane that Blanche was informed that Rochester is not quite as wealthy as she believed
  • As the fortune telling proceeds Jane realizes that the teller is not a woman at all and is in fact Rochester in disguise
  • Jane is at first upset but gets over it
  • Rochester learns from Jane that Mason has arrived at the house and is troubled by this news
  • Chapter 20
  • Later that night while Jane is attempting to sleep she hears a ruckus and cry for help
  • After rushing into the hallways Jane and the rest of the guests are told to go back to bed and the noise was merely a servant having a nightmare
  • After Jane returns to her room however she is interrupted by Rochester who tells her to accompany him to the third floor
  • On the third floor Jane learns that Mason has been stabbed in the arm, Jane is asked to stop the bleeding while they wait for a surgeon
  • Eventually the surgeon arrives to heal Mason and Jane and Rochester leave and take a walk in the orchard outside
  • While outside Rochester tells Jane a hypothetical story of a man who committed a crime abroad and for the past few years of his life has been trying to atone for his sin by seeking to alter his ways of life and marry a woman and settle down
  • Jane realizes that Rochester is telling his life story
  • Jane says that said man in the hypothetical story can only receive redemption through God
  • Rochester then asks that if marrying Blanche will bring him salvation however he does not give Jane a chance to answer
  • Chapter 21
  • Jane had previously learned that it is a bad sign to dream of children, however on seven consecutive nights Jane has dreams of babies, causing her to fear repercussions
  • Sure enough Jane learns that her cousin John has committed suicide and that her aunt has suffered a stroke
  • Jane decides to return to Gateshead where she is reunited with Bessie
  • Jane makes an attempt to repair her relationship with her dying aunt, however Mrs. Reed wants nothing to do with it
  • Jane learns that her Uncle from her fathers side had left her a letter three years earlier stating that he wished to leave her his fortune however it was hidden by her aunt
  • Jane continues to try to mend the relationship however to no avail, Mrs. Reed dies that evening
  • Chapter 22
  • Jane decides to stay at Gateshead for a whole month to comfort Georgina who does not get along very well with Eliza
  • Georgina eventually decides to move to London and live with her uncle while Eliza also decides to move away and live in a convent in France
  • The reader learns that following both of the girls moving, Eliza becomes leader of her convent and Georgina marries a wealthy man
  • Meanwhile back at Gateshead Jane learns that the guests have left Thornfield and that Rochester has left as well, he has gone off to London to buy a new carriage which suggests that he is preparing to marry Blanche
  • Jane prepares to Thornfield and wonders to herself what will become of her relationship with Rochester following his marriage
  • While on her way to Thornfield Jane runs into Rochester who shows off his new carriage and explains that he believes it will suit his new wife quite nicely, Jane tells Rochester that she is very pleased to be back with Rochester and looks forward to returning to his home
  • Chapter 23
  • After a few weeks back at Thornfield Jane has an interesting encounter with Rochester
  • The two take a walk outside of Rochester's home and Rochester explains that he is ready to marry Blanche and that he has also found Jane a new position as a governess at a home in Ireland
  • Rochester is slightly surprised however he responds by asking Jane to marry him, he explains that he only brought up Blanche to make Jane jealous and see her reaction
  • Jane is overjoyed and the new couple is very happy
  • There walk outside is suddenly interrupted by a storm and the couple rushes inside where they share their first kiss which surprises Mrs. Fairfax who witnesses it
  • That evening during the storm a bolt of lightening strikes a tree cracking it in half, Jane and Rochester had been sitting under that same tree earlier in the day
  • Jane is dismayed at the reality of being so far away from Rochester, she exclaims that being away from Rochester will greatly sadden her and she finally decides to express her love for him
  • Chapter 24
  • Preparations begin for the wedding however everything is far but smooth
  • Mrs. Fairfax is very much against the wedding as she sees Rochester and Jane's relationship as inappropriate
  • Jane also has misgivings about the marriage as she is embarrassed by her lack of wealth and how it will look when she marries Rochester
  • Jane resolves to call her uncle and ask to be named his heir so she can be on level ground with Rochester financially and feel more comfortable about the marriage
  • Chapter 25
  • The day before the wedding finally arrives and Rochester, who had been away, finally returns home
  • Jane tells Rochester about some strange events that occurred at Thornfield while he was away
  • The evening before Rochester returned Jane received her wedding dress and a veil which had been Rochester's wedding gift
  • That night while sleeping Jane has a nightmare that involved a young child lying in Jane's arms while she searched for Rochester, Jane however is unable to reach Rochester in the dream as she is stuck on a long and endless winding road
  • Jane was then suddenly awoken by a noise in her closet, she opens it to find a figure tearing her veil in half
  • Rochester explains that the dream doesn't mean anything and that the figure that tore her veil must have been Grace
  • Rochester adds that he will explain the events of that night after they have been married for a year and a day
  • That evening Jane sleeps in the same bed as Adele
  • Chapter 26
  • The day of the wedding finally comes and Sophie helps Jane get prepared
  • Jane and Rochester then head over to the church for the ceremony
  • On the way over to the church Jane notices two strangers wandering through the nearby cemetery
  • Once inside the church the two strangers appear there as well
  • During the ceremony when the preacher asks if anyone objects one of the strangers interrupts and says he objects on grounds that Rochester is already married to a Creole woman he met fifteen years earlier while living in Jamaica
  • The man announces that he is from London and his name is Mr. Briggs, he also explains that he has been sent by a man, Mr. Mason, who is the woman's brother
  • Mr. Mason proves to be the other stranger and corroborates the story
  • Rochester announces that the story is true and that in fact his second wife lives at Thornfield under the care of Grace
  • He then tells the crowd to accompany him to Thornfield so he can show them and better explain his reasoning
  • He takes the guests of the wedding to the third floor of Thornfield and shows them a room and explains that Bertha, his first wife, bit and stabbed her brother
  • He then shows them a door and another room where Bertha is being kept, Bertha immediately tries to strangle Rochester when he enters
  • After the events Jane learns from Mason that her uncle actually informed him of the marriage and that they are acquaintances
  • Following everything Jane locks herself in her room obviously disturbed, Jane cannot believe what has happened and thinks about how it will affect her life
  • Chapter 27
  • Jane awakes after a brief nap with the belief that she must leave Thornfield
  • She leaves her room and notices Rochester sitting in a chair on the threshold waiting for her
  • Rochester begs her not to leave and pleads for her forgiveness claiming that he never meant to wound her
  • Jane remains silent even though in her mind she has already resolved to forgive Rochester
  • While standing there Jane becomes very faint and Rochester carries her to the library where he attempts to revive her, Rochester has a proposal for Jane, that they move to the south of France and elope
  • Jane immediately refuses as she cannot comprehend doing such a thing while Bertha is still Alive
  • Rochester tries to argue and explain that he does not view himself as being married
  • He then begins to tell the story of his past
  • Following his father's death his father's entire estate was left to Rowland, Rochester's brother
  • Rochester was sent to Jamaica where he was intended to marry Bertha, a beautiful young woman, in order to inherit 30,000 pounds
  • Rochester convinced himself that he was in love with Bertha even though he was not in order to go through with the wedding
  • Following the marriage Rochester learns that his brother and father had urged the marriage despite knowing about Bertha's family and past because they believed it would be beneficial financially
  • Bertha's mother, who had initially been explained as being dead, turns out to still be alive and living in an insane asylum
  • As the two begin their lives together Rochester begins to realize that Bertha is very coarse and slightly crazy, often breaking into violent and loud outbursts
  • Rochester's new marriage is very hard on him and he even contemplates suicide, however he decides to instead move Bertha to Thornfield where he can isolate her
  • He then spends the next few years roaming the continent and searching for his soul mate however every woman he meets leaves him disappointed
  • That's when he meets Jane and falls in love with her
  • Jane is suddenly shocked and torn by what she has learned, she cannot make up her mind on whether to forgive him or let him suffer for what he has done
  • Jane gives Rochester a kiss on the cheek and leaves after saying she prays for him
  • That night in her sleep Jane has a dream in which her mother tells her to avoid temptation and flee
  • Jane wakes up and leaves Thornfield
  • Chapter 28
  • Jane uses the little money she has to take a coach to a nearby town, however then she is forced to sleep outside and beg for food
  • Over the next day she searches for work yet no one seems to want to help her
  • Jane wanders over to a light she sees in the distance and comes across a house in which she sees two woman and their servant
  • Jane overhears the two woman speaking and learns they are named Diana and Mary and the servant is named Hannah
  • Jane knocks on the door but the women refuse to answer causing Jane to collapse on the door step and claim she is dying
  • Eventually "St. John", the brothers of the girls, comes outside and takes in Jane
  • They offer her food and ask her questions, Jane tells them her name is "Jane Elliot"
  • Chapter 29
  • Jane spends her first few days at the Rivers' house recuperating in bed
  • On the fourth day she smells bread baking in the kitchen and ventures in to find Hannah baking
  • Jane at first criticizes Hannah for being reluctant to take her in the first night
  • Hanna apologizes and then goes on to explain her and the Rivers' story
  • The Rivers sisters Diana and Mary were both working as governesses because their father had lost all his money in a bad business deal, the two are only staying at the house now because their father recently died
  • Jane decides to tell her story and explains that she lied about her name, St. John is very moved by her story and offers to help her find a job
  • Chapter 30
  • Over the next month Jane becomes very close with the two Rivers girls, however it is fast approaching the time for them to return to their work as governesses
  • Meanwhile St. John has found Jane a job working at a charity school for young girls
  • He however suspects Jane wont last long in her work which can be attributed for his habit of being restless when it comes to his work
  • His sisters believe he will leave England to go work as a missionary overseas
  • At the end of the chapter St. John explains that their Uncle John has died however left nothing to them, instead he left everything to another
  • Jane learns that Uncle John was the one who led the Rivers' father into a bad business deal
  • Chapter 31
  • Jane moves to Morton to start her new position, she is given a cottage to live in by a rich woman Rosamond Oliver
  • Jane begins to teach and after a short while is not enjoying her job very much as the work is degrading
  • One day St. John comes to visit and he explains to her that he himself at one point realized that he made a wrong career choice, he instead decided to become a missionary
  • While the two are having their conversation Rosamond interrupts them and speaks to St. John
  • Jane believes from the two's conversation that they are in love
  • Chapter 32
  • Jane grows closer with her students through her work and her students grow closer with her as well
  • At night each day she is haunted by dreams of Rochester
  • St. John often comes to visit Jane and Rosamond specifically
  • It is quite clear to Jane that St. John has feelings for Rosamond so Jane suggests that they marry
  • St. John explains that she would not be suitable for a mission trip
  • One evening while Jane is with St. John, St. John takes notice of one of Jane's drawings
  • He tears of a corner of it suspiciously and leaves hastily, obviously noticing something that Jane does not
  • Chapter 33
  • One snowy evening while Jane is at home St. John arrives looking slightly worried
  • He explains that he is looking for a woman named Jane Eyre who was a poor governess working at Thornfield Hall before running away
  • Though St. John has not been told Jane's real name or story he suspects it to be her
  • St. John goes on to explain that it is important he find Jane Eyre because her uncle has died and left her 20,000 pounds
  • St. John explains that he had suspected Jane because of a drawing she had done that had her signature
  • He then also adds that his Uncle John who died was actually Jane's Uncle John as well and that they are in fact cousins
  • Jane decides to divide the money among her three cousins and her equally
  • Chapter 34
  • Jane decides to spend the Christmas holidays with her new cousins, however St. John acts strangely towards Jane and seems very harsh and cold
  • Jane believes it is because Rosamond has decided to marry another man
  • St. John begins to be very controlling and pushy overtime to Jane and tries to force her to marry him and travel to India to be a missionary with him
  • Jane does not want to marry him because she does not love him
  • St. John is enraged by this and storms out of the room when he hears what Jane has to say
  • Chapter 35
  • Over the next week St. John works very hard to try to convince Jane to marry him however she is still reluctant
  • However after some powerful speeches and prayer Jane is nearly swayed to marry St. John
  • One evening Jane hears what she believes to be Rochester calling for her in the distance, this phantom voice is enough to snap Jane out of her temptation to marry St. John
  • Chapter 36
  • The phantom voice is enough to compel Jane to return to Thornfield despite St. John's protests
  • She boards a coach and travels to Thornfield very excited, however she is shocked to find the house in ruins
  • Jane is frantic and travels to a nearby inn to try and learn what has happened
  • Apparently Bertha went crazy one evening and set the house on fire before killing herself
  • Rochester manages to save all the servants, however he loses a hand and his sight during the fire
  • Rochester has been moved to a home in the forest where he is cared for by his servants John and Mary
  • Chapter 37
  • Jane goes to see Rochester and while approaching his home she sees him from a distance, he looks the same but slightly disheveled and distressed
  • Jane enters the home and Rochester notices her presence however he initially believes it a ghost
  • When he learns that Jane is alive he is overjoyed and the two express their love for one another
  • They take a walk and the two agree that they shall get married
  • While on the walk Rochester tells Jane that a few nights earlier he called out for Jane in the night and he thought he heard her respond
  • Jane does not feel up to telling Rochester that she actually heard him because she is afraid it will be too much for him to handle
  • Chapter 38
  • Jane and Rochester finally are able to express their love with a private wedding ceremony
  • Jane decides to write her cousins and explain what happened, Mary and Diana write back and are both very happy, however St. John has no response
  • Jane decides to visit Adele one day and finds that she is very unhappy at her school
  • She and Rochester resolve to move her to a nicer school that will be much better for her
  • Jane then writes that it is ten years past since her visit to Adele and that her and Rochester have lived a wonderful and happy marriage
  • She tells that the two have also had a son and that by the time their son was born Rochester had regained enough vision to be able to see him
  • Jane then adds that Mary and Diana were able to both find husbands and are happily married while St. John finally decided to go on his mission trip to India like he had planned
  • Jane ends the novel with a quote from a letter St. John sent her, the quote begs for Jesus to come save him
