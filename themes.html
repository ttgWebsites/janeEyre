Themes

  • Gender inequality
  • Jane Eyre struggles throughout the novel trying to be treated as an equal
  • At this time period women are considered inferior to men and Jane has to work hard to stand up to oppression
  • Jane eventually is able to overcome many of the challenges her gender faces, however it comes through hard work and many years of standing up for herself
  • When Jane eventually marries she does so under the condition that she is equal to her husband, and that she can provide for herself
  • Social class inequality
  • Jane Eyre is a very well educated and sophisticated woman for having spent years working as a Victorian governess, however she is more or less on the level of a servant in society
  • This social distinction is frustrating to Jane as she is just as well educated and well versed as those of a higher class yet she is not accepted
  • Jane overcomes this barrier when she marries Rochester as he is from a higher social class
  • The balance between life and religion
  • Throughout the novel Jane is constantly working to find the right balance between her spiritual life and personal life
  • Jane is introduced to 3 different models of religion by 3 different people, however she rejects them all
  • She manages to put together her own ideas of a spiritual life and finds the balance she sees necessary, she focuses on morality, and she still believes in a Christian god
  • Jane finds a perfect middle ground between all the religious views pressed upon her which allows her to balance her life and achieve her desires
  • The desire to be loved without sacrificing freedom
  • One theme seen all during the novel is Jane's quest for love
  • Not only does she seek romantic love but she also seeks a sense of value from others
  • When Jane is faced with the opportunity to marry Rochester she is afraid that she will loose her freedom and be treated more like a servant to her husband
  • She hates this idea as she wants her relationship to be mutual in both feeling and value
  • Jane must wait a few years however she learns to find the perfect middle ground in her relationship as they both love and respect each other equally
