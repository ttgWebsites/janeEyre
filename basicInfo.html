  • England. Smith, Elder, and Co., Cornhill. 1847.
  • Genre
  • Romance novel, and coming of age.
  • Setting
  • The novel is set in the early 1800's and includes five locations, Jane's first home at Gateshead, Lowood School, Rochester's estate called Thornfield, Moor House where the Rivers family lives, and lastly Rochester's house in the woods of Ferndean.
  • Point Of View
  • The whole novel is narrated by Jane and she shifts from the first person describing the events of her life to the third describing them as how she recalls them throughout the novel.
  • Conflict
  • The main conflict in the novel develops after Jane meets Rochester. She has to wrestle with her own personal desires of love and comfort against her desire of being true to ones self and what is right.
