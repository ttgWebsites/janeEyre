Characters

  • Jane Eyre
  • Jane is the protagonist of the story
  • She is an orphan and is forced to grow up first with her mean aunt, however she then goes on to live and grow up at an all girls school
  • The novel follows Jane's life and growth as an independent and strong woman
  • Mrs. Reed
  • Mrs. Reed is Jane's aunt, she is put in charge of raising Jane after her parents die, however she resents Jane and does not care for her
  • She sends Jane away to an all girls school to live when she is old enough
  • John Reed
  • Jane's mean and abusive cousin
  • Eliza and Georgiana Reed
  • John Reed's sisters and Jane's cousins
  • They are both mean to Jane when she is young, however when she is older they are slightly nicer
  • Bessie
  • She is the housekeeper at the Reed household
  • She is very caring of Jane and often spends time comforting her
  • Ms. Temple
  • She is a teacher and superintendent at Lowood school and helps Jane grow and develop as a woman
  • Mr. Brocklehurst
  • He is the headmaster at Lowood school and does not care for Jane very much, he is harsh to her and embarrasses her in front of the whole school
  • Helen Burns
  • Jane's first true friend at Lowood school
  • The girls are very close and Jane learns a lot from her about life
  • Helen eventually dies which greatly saddens and distresses Jane
  • Mrs. Fairfax
  • She is a housekeeper at Thornfield, the home which Jane becomes a governess at
  • Edward Rochester
  • He is the wealthy owner of Thornfield
  • Rochester is a strange but captivating man
  • He and Jane develop a strong relationship throughout the novel and eventually marry
  • Adele Varens
  • Her mother was one of Edward Rochester's mistresses
  • Jane's role is to tutor Adele as Rochester has become her father figure after her mother died
  • Blanche Ingram
  • She is a very beautiful and wealthy woman
  • Rochester and her seem to have a fling, however they do not appear to love each other despite rumors that they will marry
  • Grace Poole
  • She is an employee at Thornfield, however she is very odd and no one seems to know quite why she is there
  • Turns out she is in charge of taking care of Bertha
  • Bertha
  • Rochester's first wife
  • Bertha and Rochester marry so Rochester can receive his inheritance, however they do not love each other
  • Bertha is crazy which forces Rochester to lock her away in his mansion
  • Rivers Siblings
  • The three siblings, Mary, Diana, and St. John take in Jane when she runs away from Thornfield
  • Mary and Diana are caring and supportive of Jane while St. John is demanding and tries to force her to marry him
  • It turns out that Jane and the siblings are cousins
